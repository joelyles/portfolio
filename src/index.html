This is a short paragraph about myself. Nothing fancy, just some simple words. I'm one of those who actually enjoys reading the instructions. No, not for everything - but I definitely value the reassurance of knowing that a new appliance or piece of furniture has been assembled correctly and that it shouldn't fail under normal use. We have all probably experienced the feeling that comes with accidentally breaking something simply because we didn't understand how all the parts were supposed to fit together.

Writing software fits under that umbrella. I enjoy solving problems and creating functionality through code. Whether I'm using React, Node JS, and the MVC pattern or plain old HTML, CSS, and JavaScript - understanding the instructions for assembly and use is crucial.

Additionally, my management experience has helped me develop an awareness and appreciation for clear, concise messaging and communication. From writing training documentation to conducting team member performance reviews to presenting metrics at the manager meeting - the proper communication of ideas and expectations produces trust and confidence for all parties. Developing software requires a mastery of the instructions.

Utilizing my aptitude toward details and effective communication is a priority of mine and I desire to contribute accordingly. If those principles resonate with you, then feel free to reach out. Check out my GitHub to see what I've been working on - github.com/joelyles.
